# UniformPass — School Discoverability & Cold-Start UX Study
Design study, not a bug hunt. Ideal journeys designed from scratch within fixed constraints: no accounts, no in-app payments, no shipping, off-platform meetup, concierge consignment as the moat. School selection is the front door to the entire product — these two situations are the two ways that front door can currently dead-end a buyer, and in a school-scoped marketplace expanding beyond 44 seeded schools, they are the single biggest growth-leak. Every dead end below is redesigned as a captured lead instead.

---

## Buyer — Their School Isn't Listed
**Goal:** Find out fast whether UniformPass works for their kid's school, and if it doesn't yet, leave having done something that moves it toward existing — not leave having hit a wall.
**Mental state / constraints:** First-time visitor, arrived with specific intent (word of mouth, a flyer, a Google search for "[school name] uniform resale"). Doesn't know only 44 schools are seeded — an empty result reads as "this app doesn't work," not "not launched here yet." High bounce risk: if they leave with nothing, they don't come back on their own. Usually on a phone, moderate patience (this isn't the urgent buyer, but it is a one-shot first impression).
**Ideal entry point:** The school search box on the homepage — full type-ahead, not a `<select>` dropdown, positioned as the first interactive element above or inside the hero, not buried in a 5-field filter bar. Also reachable via a direct `/schools/[slug]` URL from an external search engine that resolves to this same state if the slug doesn't exist.
**Ideal click-path (numbered, ≤7 steps):**
1. Land on homepage; school search is the first, most prominent action — not one of five equal-weight filters.
2. Type the school name; type-ahead live-matches against the 44 seeded schools (handles nicknames/abbreviations — "St. Joe's" → St. Joseph Regional) and ranks by proximity/state.
3. No match found — an inline "not found" panel appears directly under the search box (never a separate dead-end page), echoing back exactly what they typed: "We don't have '[query]' yet."
4. Buyer taps the panel's one primary action, "Notify me when [school] joins" — a single-field micro-form expands inline (email or phone; school name auto-attached, nothing to retype).
5. Submit → immediate confirmation: "You're on the list for [School]. We'll email you the moment it goes live," plus a live count ("3 other [School] families are already waiting").
6. Confirmation screen offers one clear next step: "Bring [School] here faster" — a pre-filled share link/message for a parent Facebook group or text thread (viral loop), and, separately, a pointer to free pickup for anyone who already has uniforms to sell regardless of whether their school is seeded yet.
7. A permanent escape hatch — "Browse nearby schools that are live" chips — sits below the fold on every step, so the buyer is never stuck with zero path forward.
**The ONE primary CTA per key screen:** Homepage → "Search your school." Not-found inline panel → "Notify me when [school] joins." Confirmation screen → "Invite your school" (share).
**Trust / clarity reinforcement points:** The exact typed school name is echoed back at every step (never a generic "no results"); a live waitlist counter per school turns a dead end into visible social proof and gentle urgency; explicit no-spam line next to the notify field ("One email, when your school goes live. Nothing else.") since this is asking for contact info with no account system to fall back on; visible scope-setting copy ("Live now across 44 NJ & NY schools — growing every month") so the buyer understands this is a expanding network, not a broken product.
**Components this journey demands (esp. school type-ahead + not-listed fallback + notify/waitlist + empty-state design):** Fuzzy/alias-aware school type-ahead (needs an `aliases` field per school row — nicknames, shorthand, common misspellings) ranked by state/proximity, debounced, keyboard-navigable; a zero-match inline panel component (distinct from the filtered-to-zero grid state) that echoes the query and never renders as a dead page; a single-field notify/waitlist micro-form with no account requirement, writing freeform school-name + contact to a `school_requests` table that doubles as mom/Dylan's expansion-priority queue; a per-school waitlist counter (aggregate count of requests, shown back to future visitors of the same not-found query); a one-tap "invite your school" share action with a pre-filled message and link; a "nearby live schools" chip list querying schools with active inventory, sorted by proximity, as a permanent fallback; a cross-sell surface into `/sell-for-me`, which already accepts a freeform "other" school — meaning a not-listed buyer can become a concierge seller lead immediately, independent of catalog completeness.
**Friction to eliminate:** A generic "no results" page with no next action; forcing account creation just to get notified; re-typing the school name across search → waitlist → confirmation; hiding or omitting the fallback to browse other live schools; silent form submission with no confirmation that the request registered; treating this like a filter problem ("try adjusting your search") when it's a coverage problem.
**Top 3 non-negotiables:**
1. A zero-match school search must always resolve into a captured lead (contact + school name saved), never a blank or dead-end screen.
2. The typed school name persists and is echoed back end-to-end, from search to waitlist confirmation — zero re-entry.
3. A live secondary action exists beyond passive waiting — invite-your-school share, or route into concierge pickup — so demand converts toward expansion now, not just banks silently.

## Empty-School Buyer — Listed, Zero Active Listings (Cold Start)
**Goal:** Confirm their school is genuinely supported (not broken/abandoned) even though there's nothing to buy yet, and either wait for inventory or personally trigger it to appear.
**Mental state / constraints:** Arrives with more hope than the not-listed buyer — they found their school — which makes the letdown of an empty grid sharper: it reads as "this app doesn't work" rather than "not launched here yet." No natural moment (like a "not found" message) explains why it's empty, so without deliberate design this is a silent bounce with zero signal captured. This is the default state for every one of the 44 seeded schools outside the 3-school NJ beachhead at launch, so it is not an edge case — it is close to the median first visit as the product expands.
**Ideal entry point:** The school-scoped browse page itself, immediately on load when listing count for that school is zero — detected server-side, not discovered by the buyer touching filters and getting "0 results."
**Ideal click-path (numbered, ≤7 steps):**
1. Buyer selects/deep-links into their school via the type-ahead.
2. Page loads and detects zero active listings for this school before any filter interaction — renders a dedicated cold-start empty state, not the generic filtered-to-zero grid.
3. Empty state states the situation plainly and positively: "[School] just joined UniformPass — no listings yet," with the concierge model explained in one line ("Most schools start this way — we personally pick up a starter batch to get things going").
4. Two equal-weight options are shown side by side: "Notify me when something's posted" (buy-side) and "Be the first to sell — request a free pickup" (sell-side).
5a. Notify path: single-field inline form (email/phone, school pre-attached) → instant confirmation with a live waitlist count ("12 families waiting for [School]").
5b. Sell path: routes directly into `/sell-for-me` with the school field pre-filled — no re-selecting school, one less form to fill.
6. After either path, a permanent fallback stays visible: "Browse nearby schools with listings now" — chips linking to schools with live inventory (e.g., the 3-school beachhead), so the session still produces something to look at.
7. Buyer exits with a waitlist confirmation, a pickup request in motion, or lands in a comparable school's active inventory — never a static "0 items, try adjusting filters" screen.
**The ONE primary CTA per key screen:** Cold-start empty state → "Notify me when something's posted" (the buyer's own intent stays primary; "Request a free pickup" sits as an equally visible but secondary action). Notify confirmation → "Browse nearby schools with listings." Sell-for-me hand-off → "Request pickup" (native primary of that page, now pre-filled).
**Trust / clarity reinforcement points:** State is explicitly labeled "New school" / "Just joined," never rendered as a bare empty grid that implies failure; a visible waitlist count doubles as social proof for buyers and as a recruiting nudge for anyone reading who could sell ("12 families are waiting for someone to sell"); the seller invitation is framed as opportunity, not obligation ("most schools sell out their first few listings within days"); the concierge/free-pickup explanation stays visible in the empty state so it reads as pre-launch momentum, not neglect.
**Components this journey demands (esp. school type-ahead + not-listed fallback + notify/waitlist + empty-state design):** Server-side cold-start detection at the school-page level (`listing count === 0` triggers a dedicated component, entirely distinct from the filter-driven "no results, adjust filters" state); a dual-path empty-state component (notify-me + sell-for-me nudge) with school context auto-attached to both branches; the same notify/waitlist form used in the not-listed situation, reused here pre-filled with an existing `school_id` instead of freeform text; a per-school demand counter shared across both notify and empty-state contexts (same underlying `school_requests`/interest table); a "nearby schools with inventory" chip list (query: schools where active listing count > 0, sorted by proximity); a deep link from the empty state straight into `/sell-for-me?school_id=X` that pre-fills and locks the school field.
**Friction to eliminate:** Generic "no listings found — try adjusting your filters" copy that implies buyer error instead of describing a genuine cold-start; forcing the buyer back to `/sell-for-me` to re-pick a school already selected once; no visible distinction between "filtered to zero" and "zero listings, period"; an empty state with no forward path (guaranteed churn); treating seller-recruitment as a footnote when it is the only mechanism that actually resolves the empty state.
**Top 3 non-negotiables:**
1. Zero total listings must render a distinct, purpose-built empty state — never the generic "0 results, adjust filters" message used for over-filtered searches.
2. The empty state carries equal-weight buy-side (notify me) and sell-side (free pickup) CTAs, since fixing a cold-start school requires converting the same visiting audience into its first sellers.
3. Any hand-off into consignment/sell-for-me from an empty school state carries the school context forward automatically — zero re-entry, one less reason to abandon the flow.
