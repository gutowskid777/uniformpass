## Buyer — First-timer, unsure it's legit
**Goal:** Find a specific uniform item (or browse their kid's school) and walk away confident enough to message a seller — without ever wondering "is this a scam."

**Mental state / constraints:** Landed cold (link from a school parent Facebook group, PTA email, or search) with zero prior brand recognition. Actively scanning for red flags: no "About," no pricing transparency, no way to see who's behind it = bounce. They will NOT create an account, will NOT read a long FAQ, and will NOT tolerate ambiguity about where their money or contact info goes. They are evaluating the platform's legitimacy in parallel with evaluating the product — legitimacy comes first, or the product never gets a look. Mobile, distracted, comparing against "I could just ask in the Facebook group instead."

**Ideal entry point:** School-specific landing page (e.g. `uniformpass.com/schools/newark-academy`) reached via a link shared by the school/PTA — NOT the generic homepage. The page must immediately confirm "yes, this is for MY kid's school" (school name + crest/colors in the header) and answer legitimacy in the first viewport: a one-line "what this is" statement, a visible "How it works" entry, and the Verified badge explained inline near the first product grid — before any single item detail page.

**Ideal click-path (numbered, ≤7 steps):**
1. Land on school landing page → see school name confirmed, one-line trust statement ("Local parent-run marketplace for [School] uniforms — no accounts, no payments on site, you meet the seller directly"), and a visible "How it works" link/strip.
2. Skim the "How it works" strip (3 icons: Browse → Contact seller directly → Meet & pay in person) without leaving the page — builds the no-money-through-platform model as a feature, not a workaround.
3. Browse grid, filtered to their school by default; each card shows price, size, condition, and a small "Verified" badge on operator-inspected items.
4. Click into an item → see full photos, condition notes, seller type (parent seller vs. "Sold by UniformPass" concierge), and — critically — a restated one-line safety note near the CTA ("You'll contact the seller directly to arrange a time/place — UniformPass never handles payment").
5. Click "Who runs this?" or "About" link (persistent in footer/nav) if still hesitant → short, human page: founder note, NJ-based, which schools are live, how items get Verified, meetup safety tips (public place, daytime, bring a friend).
6. Click the single primary CTA — "Contact Seller" (or "Ask About This Item") → lightweight modal explains exactly what happens next (opens email/text template, no account needed, seller responds directly) before committing.
7. Send the message → confirmation screen reinforces the safety framing once more ("You're all set — coordinate a meetup time and place directly with the seller. Tip: public locations work best.") and offers to browse more.

**The ONE primary CTA per key screen:**
- School landing page: "Browse [School] Uniforms"
- Product grid: (no single CTA — browsing) implicit "View Item"
- Item detail page: "Contact Seller"
- Contact modal: "Send Message"
- Post-contact confirmation: "Keep Browsing"

**Trust / clarity reinforcement points (be exhaustive — this is the core of this persona):**
- Header/hero: school name + crest so it's unmistakably "for me."
- One-line model explainer above the fold: no accounts, no payment on-site, meet in person — framed as privacy/simplicity, not a workaround or limitation.
- "How it works" 3-step strip visible without scrolling far, repeated/linked in footer on every page.
- Verified badge with a tooltip or inline micro-explainer on first appearance ("Verified = inspected in person by UniformPass before listing — condition and authenticity confirmed").
- Persistent "About / Who runs this" link in primary nav, not buried in a footer wall of links.
- About page names real humans, states NJ base, lists participating schools (44 schools = social proof of scale/legitimacy), explains the concierge "Sell it for me" model plainly.
- Seller type disclosure on every listing (parent-listed vs. UniformPass-inspected/concierge) — sets expectations before contact.
- Explicit "what happens when you click Contact" preview before the click commits them (no surprise account creation, no surprise payment screen).
- Safety/meetup guidance (public place, daytime, bring someone) surfaced at the moment of contact, not buried in a help center.
- No dark patterns: no fake urgency, no countdown timers, no "3 people viewing this" — a first-timer sniffing for scams will treat marketplace-hype tactics as a red flag, not a nudge.
- Consistent, calm visual design (not a bare-bones template) — visual polish itself signals "someone maintains this."
- No request for payment info, card entry, or account creation anywhere in the flow — the absence itself is the proof point, but it should also be said out loud once.

**Components this journey demands (e.g. how-it-works strip, Verified explainer, safety/meetup tips, about/who-we-are, social proof):**
- School-specific landing header (crest, name, live-school count)
- "How it works" 3-step strip (persistent, linked from nav/footer)
- Verified badge + inline explainer (tooltip or micro-modal on tap)
- About / Who We Are page (real names, NJ basis, school count, concierge model explained)
- Seller-type disclosure component on listings (parent vs. concierge/Verified)
- Contact-flow preview modal ("here's what happens next")
- Safety & meetup tips module (shown at contact step and linked from About)
- Social proof strip (number of schools, optionally parent testimonial quotes — no fake counters)
- Persistent, low-key trust footer (About, How it Works, Safety Tips, Contact Us) on every page

**Friction to eliminate:**
- Any login/signup wall, even a soft "create an account to contact seller" prompt.
- Ambiguity about what happens after clicking "Contact" — must be previewed, not a surprise.
- Generic homepage detour before reaching their school — go straight to school-specific context.
- Legitimacy content buried in a footer FAQ instead of surfaced inline at the moment of doubt.
- Any implication that UniformPass touches payment (progress bars, "checkout," cart icons, price totals with tax/shipping — none of that vocabulary belongs here).
- Long-form legalese as the only trust-building content — needs a human, plain-language "who we are," not just Terms of Service.

**Top 3 non-negotiables:**
1. The no-accounts/no-payment model must be stated as a deliberate, positive design choice within the first viewport — not discovered by absence three screens later.
2. The Verified badge must be self-explanatory on first sight (inline, no hunting), because it's the platform's core trust signal and moat.
3. A real "who's behind this" (About) page must be one click away from any screen, and must feel human (names, NJ roots, school count) rather than corporate boilerplate.
