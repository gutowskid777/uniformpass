## 3. Sequencing + cadence (don't spam, don't stall)

**Order matters: land SUPPLY first, then DEMAND.** An empty store converts nobody. Get 2-3 real piles listed before you push buyers hard.

| Day | Do this | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1 (today)** | Fill in the map above. Text your 3-4 warmest SUPPLY people (consignment pitch a1). | 3-4 texts |
| **Day 2** | Text next 4-5 SUPPLY people. Confirm any pickups from Day 1. List that inventory, flag it Verified in admin. | 4-5 texts + list |
| **Day 3** | Post in group #1 (your strongest / your mom's group). Start DEMAND texts (a2) to 3-4 people. | 1 post + 3-4 texts |
| **Day 4** | Post in group #2. More DEMAND texts. Send 48h follow-ups (c) to Day-1/2 non-responders. | 1 post + 3-4 texts + follow-ups |
| **Day 5** | Post in group #3 (if warm). Follow up remaining. Close pickups + hand off first sales. | 1 post + follow-ups |
| **Weekend** | Do the pickups. Take good photos. List everything. Tally the tracker. | pickups + listing |

**Anti-spam guardrails:**
- Max ~5 fresh 1:1 texts per day. Personalize each... use their kid's name.
- One group post per group, ever (this week). Space group posts a day apart.
- One follow-up per person, max. Silence = no. Move on.
- Never paste the same message to multiple groups back-to-back.

## 4. The concierge-consignment pitch script (the wedge)

This is the moat. When someone bites on supply, or you're talking to a parent in person, run this. Keep it to 3 beats.

**Beat 1 — the pain (they already feel it):**
> "You've got a pile of [school] uniforms [kid]'s grown out of, right? They're too good to toss and a pain to sell."

**Beat 2 — the offer (do-nothing + 50%):**
> "Here's the deal: I come grab the whole pile. I photograph it, list it, handle every buyer message, and arrange the meetup. When it sells, you keep 50%. You don't lift a finger."

**Beat 3 — the close (make it concrete):**
> "I can swing by [this week / Saturday]. Just box up whatever [kid]'s done with. Want me to come get it?"

**Handling the 3 objections:**
- *"How much will I actually get?"* → "Depends what's in the pile and condition, but you keep half of whatever it sells for. Zero effort for found money on stuff you were going to give away."
- *"Is this legit / who buys it?"* → "Other [school] parents. Same idea as Facebook Marketplace, just scoped to our schools so it actually sells. I do all the work."
- *"I don't have time to deal with it."* → "That's the whole point... you don't. You put a box by the door, I take it from there."

**If they'd rather DIY:** point them to `https://uniformpass.shop` to post it themselves. A listing is still a win.

**After pickup (your job, not theirs):** photograph clean, list under the right school, flag **Verified by UniformPass** in `/admin` so cold buyers trust it.
